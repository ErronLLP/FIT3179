Why study Australian Bushfires?

The Australian Bushfires 2019-2020 refers to a series of hill fires that will start burning across Australia in September 2019 due to hot and dry weather.

The University of Sydney estimates that over one billion animals have been killed by the ongoing fires across the country.

Therefore, understanding the spatial and temporal information about the occurrence of the Australian bushfires is useful for future fire prevention.

What does "Number of Fire" tell us?

Unlike the northern hemisphere, August is winter in Australia and also the country's dry season.

Almost all of the Australian outback is desert land, with a huge difference in temperature between day and night, and the dry conditions are very prone to bushfires.

Understanding the variation in the number of fires can help experts delve deeper into the causes of fires. We can see that the number of fires is not stable from day to day, rather the number varies greatly, which may suggest there are other factors relate to time that causes the fires.

What does "Fire pixel brightness" tell us?

Data on forest fires are obtained from remote sensing satellites detecting infrared brightness, so pixel brightness is obtained as an indicator of temperature; the higher the pixel brightness, the more severe the fire is in an area.

This pie chart shows a smaller proportion of low severity (K = [280,300])> and high severity fires (K = [360,380]) as a proportion of total fires, with a more consistent proportion of medium severity (K = [300,320] , [320,340], [340,360]) fires.
